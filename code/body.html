Regular car maintenance is crucial for preventing breakdowns and ensuring your vehicle runs smoothly. This includes oil changes, checking fluid levels, inspecting brakes, and monitoring tire pressure. These simple tasks can extend the life of your car and improve its performance on the road.

When your car starts making strange noises or exhibiting unusual behavior, diagnosing the issue is key. Mechanics use diagnostic tools to read error codes from the car's computer and perform tests to pinpoint the problem. This could range from electrical issues to mechanical failures that require immediate attention.

Engine troubles are among the most complex and expensive to fix. Mechanics diagnose engine problems through a series of tests including compression tests, spark plug inspection, and checking the fuel system. Repairs may involve replacing worn-out components, fixing leaks, or even rebuilding the engine in severe cases.
